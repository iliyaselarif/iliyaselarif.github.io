Tips for Stock Market Investing

  • Diversifying your portfolio: It is extremely importing when investing in general to diversify your portfolio. The ever-changing economy makes many sectors rise or fall together, so you never want to have all of your eggs in one basket because of the chance that whatever your investments are in are all related and move together.
  • Riding a winner: One fundamental stock market tactic is "riding" a stock, which essentially means finding a stock that is rapidly increasing in price and hopping on the wave. Many investors do not know the point at which the stock has hit a relative ceiling so they continue to ride the winner strictly based on momentum and no fundamentals. As a new investor this is a great way to get your feet wet and to get a sense as to how a stock's price moves throughout the day. Many times you are able to cash in a generous profit simply by just riding a trend like this, without knowing anything about the company itself. Using this strategy, it is important that you do not get greedy, as mentioned before, no one knows the tipping point of the stock, so if you are one that is riding a stock strictly based on this "riding a winner" philosophy, cash out while you can.
  • Selling a Loser: There is no guarantee that a stock will rebound after a protracted decline, and it’s important to be realistic about the prospect of poorly-performing investments. And even though acknowledging losing stocks can psychologically signal failure, there is no shame recognizing mistakes and selling off investments to stem further loss. This concept is extremely important as related to the previous tip of riding a winner, many ride losers and will continue to sell stocks that are declining in value. If you are not quick to get out of a losing position, you could end up losing more and more money.
  • Don't Overemphasize the P/E Ratio: Investors often place great importance on price-earnings ratios, but placing too much emphasis on a single metric is ill-advised. P/E ratios are best used in conjunction with other analytical processes. Therefore a low P/E ratio doesn't necessarily mean a security is undervalued, nor does a high P/E ratio necessarily mean a company is overvalued.
  • Focus on the Future: Investing requires making informed decisions based on things that have yet to happen. Past data can indicate things to come, but it’s never guaranteed. It’s important to invest based on future potential versus past performance.
  • Be Open-Minded: Many great companies are household names, but many good investments lack brand awareness. Furthermore, thousands of smaller companies have the potential to become the blue-chip names of tomorrow. In fact, small-caps stocks have historically shown greater returns than their large-cap counterparts. From 1926 to 2001, small-cap stocks in the U.S. returned an average of 12.27% while the Standard & Poor's 500 Index (S&P 500) returned 10.53%. This is not to suggest that you should devote your entire portfolio to small-cap stocks. But there are many great companies beyond those in the Dow Jones Industrial Average (DJIA).
  • Hire a Pro: Finding a professional financial adviser that fits your needs. Some feel that they are better off to pay a professional to manage and invest their money rather then spending the time doing the research themselves. Some may have a job that it is more valuable to work more hours then to spend those extra hours that they could be working but rather doing research on what to invest in.

Tips for Real Estate Investing

  • Having a plan: Many real estate investors go into the field without a concrete plan, which often is the reason many investors do not see success. Knowing whether you prefer steady income, holding and letting your investment grow, or just quick flipping is the first step in becoming successful in the real estate field. An investor that would rather have steady income will most likely choose to invest in something like an apartment building where he can depend on steady income every month from investors whereas someone who doesnt mind having some of their money tied up may choose to buy a residential home and hold onto it with the intent of selling it in the future when the property gains value. Having a plan and knowing what your goals are is the first step in the real estate investing process.
  • Knowing the market: Effective real estate investors acquire an in-depth knowledge of their selected market(s). Keeping tabs on current trends, including any changes in consumer spending habits, mortgage rates, and the unemployment rate, and others, lets real estate investors acknowledge current conditions and plan for the future. This enables them to predict when trends may change, creating potential opportunities for the prepared investor.

Mastering one field allows you to become more profitable and enter different niches. A common mistake is to try and invest in all types of real estate before developing a deep understanding of one specific field. Once you develop an understanding of one field and have success in investing in that field, it allows you to gain more confidence when investing in something new and it allows you to start knowing why you should be diversifying your real estate investments because the field you have an understanding of may not be the right investment at the time.

Tips for Cash and Bond Market Investing

  • Define your objectives: Is your investment objective to have enough money for your child's college education? Is your goal to live comfortably in retirement? If so, how comfortably? You probably have multiple goals. Lay them all out and be as precise as you can. Remember: If you don't know where you're going, you'll never arrive.
  • Assess your risk profile: Different bonds and bond funds, like stocks and stock funds, carry different risk profiles. Always know the risks before you invest. It's a good idea to write them down so they are all in plain sight.
  • Don't try to time the market: Avoid speculating on interest rates. Decisions are too often made on where rates have been rather than where they are going. Instead, stick to the investment strategy that will best help you achieve your goals and objectives.
  • Know what bonds match your goals: Knowing what bonds offer what is the first step to investing in the bond market. Some bonds pay higher coupons so they would be better for someone looking for steady yearly income, whereas some bonds don't pay any coupon but accumulate price growth and are better for someone saving money for retirement.
  • Plan to reinvest your coupons: This allows the power of compounding to work on your behalf. It's a good idea to establish a "coupon account" before you start receiving coupons, so that you have a place to save the money and are not tempted to spend it.
